About Me

Technology innovations won't improve public welfare by its own, but rather following the trend of our economy. Being part of a young and skilled generation I want to use this very dynamic moment of digital transformation to improve ways of living for the better. This century, we need to begin rethinking our role between technological evolution and ecological boundaries.

My educational career started with the interest for organizational processes and urban economic development, which is why I got my degree in economics and sociology. During my studies I got fascinated by the backbone institutions of our welfare (public administration, mobility infrastructure, health-, energy- and educational system, etc.). I believe that these institutions can and need to be decoupled from the unpredictable outcomes of economic fluctuations and hardened to withstand possible threads of natural or human-caused attacks and catastrophes. In Germany however, it is these institutions in which digitalization, innovation and security lacks the most, allowing future scientists and technicians to entry a open area of fruitful research and work to be done.

However, with the beginning of the Corona Crisis, I found myself unprepared for the todays requirement of technological knowledge. Being the first generation of my family who seeks an academic carrer, I wanted to fill in the gap and apparently found deep fascination for computers and web technology. Now, 3 years later after the Pandemic started, my heart jumps higher for big vision of Smart Cities, IT-Infrastructure, E-Gouvernance and Innovations in Web Technology. I'm more than happy about my decision and continue following my story into the visionary field of IT.
